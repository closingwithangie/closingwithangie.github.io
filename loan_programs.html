First-Time Buyers

Never owned a home? These programs are designed to get you in with the least amount of money down and the most support.

  • FHA Loans — 3.5% down, 580+ credit
  • Conventional 97 — 3% down
  • USDA — 0% down in eligible rural areas
  • VA — 0% down for veterans & active duty
  • Down Payment Assistance (DPA) programs
  • NC Housing Finance Agency programs
Low down payment options available

Programs Worth Knowing About

A few that agents often don't know exist

🏗️
Construction
One-Time Close Construction Loans
Build and finance in one loan — one closing, one set of closing costs. The loan converts automatically to a permanent mortgage when construction is complete. Perfect for buyers who want to build new.
📊
Investor Favorite
DSCR Loans — No Personal Income Needed
Debt Service Coverage Ratio loans qualify based on the property's rental income, not the buyer's personal income. Great for investors who write off a lot or have complex income. If the rent covers the mortgage — they can qualify.
🏠
Renovation
FHA 203k & Fannie Mae HomeStyle
Buy a fixer-upper and finance the repairs in the same loan. The after-improved value is used for the appraisal — so buyers can purchase a home that needs work and build instant equity. Opens up inventory that other buyers skip.
🌾
Commercial & Agricultural
Commercial, Farm & Agricultural Loans
Multi-family, churches, office, retail, hotel, industrial, mixed use, hobby farms, barndominiums, dairy farms, vineyards, and rural land. If it's a property someone wants to finance — there's probably a program for it.
💊
Professional
Doctor Loan Program
Designed for medical professionals with high student debt but strong income potential. Student loans treated differently in DTI calculation. Low to no down payment options with no PMI. Also available for dentists, veterinarians, and other licensed professionals.
🔑
Flexible
Down Payment Assistance (DPA)
Multiple DPA programs available that can cover 3–5% of the purchase price as a grant or forgivable loan. Many buyers qualify and don't know it. Ask Angie to run a DPA eligibility check — it takes 10 minutes and could save thousands at closing.
